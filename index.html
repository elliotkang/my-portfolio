    Elastomeric Composite 3D Printer

    4 mechanical engineering students were tasked with designing a 3D printer that would be able to extrude elastomers which are a viscous, thermoset liquid in its initial phase. The purpose of the device is to aid in research of specific elastomers which are enhanced with nanoparticles such as graphene. The addition of these nanoparticles introduces new mechanical properties to the elastomer like increased strength, thermal conductivity, and electroconductivity. The team worked to create a modification to the Ender 3 V2 printer that transforms its typical filament style material feed into one that utilizes a positive displacement peristaltic pump to feed elastomeric composites.
